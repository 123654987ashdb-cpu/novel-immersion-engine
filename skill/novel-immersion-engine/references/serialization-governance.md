# Serialization Governance

Use this reference for continuing novels, long web-fiction runs, multi-session projects, and any chapter whose success depends on controlling promises, short-term direction, repetition, resources, or subplots.

## Author Intent And Current Focus

Keep long-horizon identity separate from near-term steering.

```markdown
# Author Intent
- Core experience this novel must preserve:
- Central question or wound:
- Non-negotiable emotional/ethical direction:
- Ending gravity, even if the route changes:
- Elements the author does not want:

# Current Focus
- Applies to chapters:
- Immediate reader pull:
- Character or relationship emphasis:
- Required payoff or pressure increase:
- What must remain unrevealed:
- Temporary restraint or tonal instruction:
- Why this focus serves Author Intent:
```

Rules:

- Change `Current Focus` freely as the serial develops.
- Change `Author Intent` only after the user explicitly redirects the novel.
- Do not confuse a short-term delay, POV choice, or tonal shift with a rewrite of the whole story.

## Authority And Active Overrides

Resolve conflicts before drafting. Use the highest applicable layer.

| Priority | Layer | Examples | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Accepted prose and confirmed canon | occurred event, established rule, witnessed object transfer | Do not contradict without an explicit repair or retcon. |
| 2 | Explicit author intent | desired core experience, ending gravity, prohibited direction | Future planning must serve it unless the user changes it. |
| 3 | Current focus and direct local instruction | next chapters emphasize loss, delay reveal, shift POV | May alter selected plans locally; cannot silently rewrite priority 1-2. |
| 4 | Selected future route | approved outline, volume plan, intended payoff | Guides drafting until revised or contradicted by accepted prose. |
| 5 | Candidate or inferred material | variants, speculative explanations, draft discoveries | Never treat as truth without acceptance. |

```markdown
## Active Override Record
| ID | Conflict | Higher authority | Temporarily changed plan | Scope/chapters | Reason | Requires later repair? | Approved by |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

If two instructions conflict at the same authority level, surface the choice or label a conservative assumption. Never hide the conflict in prose.

## Chapter Execution Packet

For ongoing or complex work, compile this packet only after the relevant volume-stage skeleton has been selected or reconstructed. It is an internal planning control sheet before generating a stage-guided chapter writing blueprint, not material to dump into the external prose prompt.

```markdown
## Chapter Execution Packet
- Chapter:
- Authority checked through chapter:
- Selected route/version:
- Active current focus:
- Dominant chapter function:
- Reader question entering:
- Payoff due now:
- State that must change:

### Must Keep
- Canon facts:
- Character/relationship constraints:
- Knowledge boundaries:
- Key object/ability or item-effect/resource conditions:
- Voice constraints:

### Must Avoid
- Contradiction or unearned reveal:
- Theme statement the characters cannot earn:
- Repeated beat/ending to avoid:
- New promise or object not justified:

### Agenda
- Hook/promises to advance, resolve, defer, or avoid opening:
- Subplot to touch or intentionally leave dormant:
- Ability/item-effect, resource, and core-cost movement:
- Cadence change from recent chapters:

### Source Trace
| Constraint or task | Source: chapter/user decision/ledger | Why active now |
| --- | --- | --- |
```

The source trace need not be shown in the chapter writing blueprint. Maintain it on the planning side when continuing old prose, managing many chapters, or resolving continuity risk.

## Promise Debt And Hook Governance

A setup is a promise debt once readers are meant to remember or anticipate it. Do not only plant hooks; schedule their progress and prevent debt from becoming noise.

Statuses:

- `proposed`: candidate only, not yet planted.
- `open`: planted and alive.
- `progressing`: changed meaning, pressure, or evidence in a later beat.
- `deferred`: intentionally dormant with a return point.
- `resolved`: paid off with consequence.
- `retired`: intentionally removed or made irrelevant with an explained reason.

Timing classes:

- `immediate`: within the chapter or next chapter.
- `near-term`: within the current mini-arc.
- `mid-arc`: within the volume or major arc.
- `slow-burn`: maintained through several arcs.
- `endgame`: integral to final resolution.

```markdown
## Promise Debt Board
| ID | Promise/hook family | Status | Timing | Planted | Last meaningful advance | Debt risk | Next required action | Planned payoff consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Current Hook Agenda
- Must advance now:
- Eligible to resolve now:
- Intentionally deferred and why:
- Hook families not to duplicate:
- New hook allowance: open / restricted / none
- Reason:
```

Rules:

- Count meaningful advancement, not mere mentions.
- Warn when a promise has been untouched beyond its expected timing.
- Restrict new hook families when unresolved debt is already crowding the reader's attention.
- Set a project-specific active-hook capacity based on genre, chapter length, and reader load; do not impose one universal hard number.
- A payoff must change action, relationship, status, belief, danger, cost, or interpretation, not merely explain a clue.

## Recent Cadence Trail

Use this to stop serialization from repeating the same emotional motion, location container, reward, or chapter ending by accident.

```markdown
## Recent Cadence Trail
| Chapter | Function | Dominant mood | Primary setting/container | Reward type | Ending type/image | Core-cost signal | Repetition risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Cadence Direction For Next Chapter
- Pattern worth continuing deliberately:
- Pattern now exhausted:
- Change required in function, mood, container, reward, or ending:
- Why the change still serves the current arc:
```

Repetition may be powerful when escalation or ritual is intentional. If the meaning is not changing, break the pattern.

## Countable Resource Ledger

Use alongside the key-object ledger when scarcity, expenditure, measurable contamination, countdowns, favors, medication, ammunition, money, access, reputation, or power reserve affects choices.

```markdown
## Resource Ledger
| Resource | Unit/rule | Opening state | Chapter delta | Closing state | Cause/source | Who knows | Plot consequence | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Rules:

- Track quantities only when they constrain action or reader expectation.
- Pair a quantified emotional or bodily cost with visible lived evidence.
- Do not silently restore spent resources or alter measurement rules.
- Mark uncertain extraction from old prose as `unverified` until confirmed.

## Subplot Board

Use for any side line expected to recur: supporting characters, faction moves, family pressure, romance complications, investigations, public opinion, rival progression, or hidden agendas.

```markdown
## Subplot Board
| Thread | Owner/characters | Function for main story | Current state | Last advance | Next re-entry window | Dormant intentionally? | Risk if forgotten |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

A subplot can be quiet. It cannot vanish unintentionally after promising consequence.

## Governed Chapter Cycle

For a continuing project:

1. Read the authoritative current-story state, `Author Intent`, and `Current Focus`.
2. Locate the current work within the selected `小说总纲 -> 分卷纲 -> 卷内阶段纲 -> 章节纲`; create or repair the missing current-stage layer first.
3. Resolve authority conflicts and record any active override.
4. Check promise debt, recent cadence, abilities/item effects, resources, subplots, core cost, objects, voice, and information boundaries across the active stage.
5. Compile the internal Chapter Execution Packet for target chapter(s).
6. Build chapter contracts and scene cards, then distill a compact stage navigation plus fixed-format `章节写作蓝图` from `chapter-writing-blueprint.md`.
7. Give that `阶段正文交接包` and minimal necessary continuity/voice material to the external prose model.
8. Receive the prose result and run the draft acceptance check against the blueprint and canon chapter by chapter.
9. If accepted, write back a state delta, hook/cadence/ability/resource/subplot movements, and next inheritance.
10. If validation fails, preserve old canon and mark disputed changes for repair or confirmation.
11. When one planned stage has been fully accepted, or two adjacent stages have returned at the latest, run the `Completed Stage Checkpoint` from `project-state-and-production.md`.
12. Preserve accepted prose, then recalibrate any unwritten stage outlines, chapter roles, promise timing, or next handoff affected by the checkpoint; if a volume or novel-level endpoint is threatened, stop for a route decision.
