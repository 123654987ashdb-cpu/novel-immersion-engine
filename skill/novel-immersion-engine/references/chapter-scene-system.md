# Chapter And Scene System

Use this reference when turning a novel bible or outline into chapter-level design, detailed outlines, and scene cards that feed a chapter writing blueprint.

For an ongoing serial or complex continuation, first confirm the target chapter's role in the selected volume stage from `hierarchical-outline-system.md`, compile the internal Chapter Execution Packet in `serialization-governance.md`, then export the stage-guided handoff through `chapter-writing-blueprint.md`.

## Chapter Function Table

Each chapter needs a dominant function plus at least one visible reader reward.

```markdown
| Chapter | Arc position | Dominant function | Opening hook | Reader question | Main pressure | Character change | Relationship change | Setup/payoff | Reader reward | Ending hook | Cannot-delete reason |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Dominant chapter functions:

- Action: threat, tactic, injury, cost.
- Discovery: clue, misread, evidence, deeper question.
- Relationship: trust, intimacy, betrayal, boundary, dependence.
- Strategy: plan, counter-plan, tradeoff, hidden risk.
- Setback: loss, consequence, status drop, moral cost.
- Reveal: answer, reframing, new danger.
- Aftermath: emotional accounting, new decision, changed bond.
- Progression: limit, test, breakthrough, stronger problem.
- Social/status: humiliation, recognition, leverage, public reversal.

Avoid placing too many chapters with the same function in sequence unless the repetition itself escalates.

## Reader Reward Menu

Use one main reward and one smaller reward in most chapters.

- **Information reward**: a truth, clue, rule, identity, motive, or hidden connection becomes visible.
- **Emotional reward**: pressure releases, grief lands, affection breaks through, anger finally acts.
- **Relationship reward**: a bond shifts, a boundary breaks, trust is earned, debt is created.
- **Competence reward**: the character shows skill, strategy, courage, control, or growth under pressure.
- **Status reward**: recognition, humiliation reversal, public victory, leverage, rank, resource.
- **Choice reward**: the character makes a costly decision that closes an old path.
- **Aesthetic reward**: an image, sentence, scene, or motif creates lingering impact while still serving story function.
- **Dread reward**: the reader realizes danger is larger, closer, stranger, or more intimate than expected.
- **Loss-progress reward**: the reader receives a concrete sign that a central erosion, sacrifice, transformation, or countdown has advanced.


Scene turn types:

- Decision.
- Reveal.
- Reversal.
- Loss.
- Cost made visible.
- Relationship boundary crossed.
- New threat.
- Old plan fails.
- Status changes.
- Desire changes.

## Beat Ladder

Use this for scene sequencing before distilling the chapter writing blueprint.

```markdown
1. Anchor image or action near disturbance.
2. Immediate desire becomes visible.
3. First resistance blocks the easy path.
4. Character uses a tactic.
5. Complication, clue, refusal, or unexpected behavior changes the problem.
6. Pressure narrows options.
7. Character chooses, fails, pays, learns, reveals, or crosses a line.
8. Exit state creates the next pull.
```

## Dialogue Design

Before writing important dialogue, define:

```markdown
## Conversation
- Surface topic:
- Hidden topic:
- Speaker A wants:
- Speaker B wants:
- A's defense:
- B's defense:
- What A will not say:
- What B will not say:
- Power at start:
- Power at end:
- Last line effect:
- Thematic boundary: what meaning must remain unsaid and be shown through consequence:
```

Line functions:

- Probe, dodge, attack, flatter, confess, threaten, joke, test, comfort, bargain, redirect, withdraw, expose, protect.

Cut dialogue that only explains facts the writer wants the reader to know. Put exposition inside conflict, negotiation, accusation, instruction, taboo, or consequence.

Do not make a character deliver the author's interpretation of the novel. Important characters can say beliefs they have earned, feared, rationalized, or misunderstood; the final thematic force should land through what happens, what is lost, what remains unsaid, and what returns with changed meaning.

## Paragraph And Sentence Function

Check prose from larger function downward.

```markdown
## Paragraph Function Check
- Paragraph job:
- Scene function served:
- New information/action/pressure:
- Character or relationship effect:
- Keep, cut, merge, or rewrite:
```

Sentence functions:

- Action.
- Perception.
- Reaction.
- Tactic.
- Consequence.
- Clue.
- Setup or reminder.
- Rhythm break.
- Transition.
- Image that carries pressure, character, world, or theme.
- Dialogue move.

Do not force every sentence to be loud. Quiet sentences are valid when they control rhythm, orientation, aftershock, dread, intimacy, or contrast.

## Anti-Filler Rules

- If a scene ends where it began, rewrite the turn.
- If a paragraph repeats a known emotion, replace it with a new action, cost, clue, or choice.
- If a beautiful sentence does not alter attention, pressure, voice, or meaning, cut or attach it to function.
- If exposition arrives before need, move it to the moment it creates a problem.
- If a chapter only sets up, add a mini-payoff.
- If a chapter only pays off, open a new question.
- If unresolved promises are already stalled, advance or pay one before opening a new hook family.
- If recent chapters repeat the same function, mood, reward, or ending, change the cadence unless repetition now transforms meaning.
- If a limited resource or countable cost changes, record its before/after state and consequence.
- If a skill or item effect solves pressure, confirm its source, boundary, cost and prior setup; otherwise repair the beat.
- If a cliffhanger resolves with no consequence, remove or replace it.
- If a character speaks from knowledge they could not possess, either plant the source of knowledge or rewrite the beat.
- If a line states the book's meaning instead of dramatizing it, replace it with action, omission, echo, consequence, or a character-limited belief.
