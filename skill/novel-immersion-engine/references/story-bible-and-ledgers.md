# Story Bible And Ledgers

Use these templates when the user is building or maintaining a long-form novel. Keep entries compact and functional. Expand only the ledgers that affect the current task.

For the required progression from `小说总纲` through `分卷纲`, `卷内阶段纲`, `章节纲`, and `章节细纲/章节写作蓝图`, use `hierarchical-outline-system.md`. The bible and ledgers support that hierarchy; they do not replace it.

## Setup And Payoff Ledger

Every major reveal, twist, object, rule, phrase, and contradiction should have a ledger entry.

```markdown
## Setup
- ID:
- Item, clue, phrase, behavior, rule, absence, or contradiction:
- First plant location:
- Reader's likely interpretation:
- True meaning:
- Reminder beats:
- Expected timing: immediate / near-term / mid-arc / slow-burn / endgame
- Last meaningful advance:
- Payoff location:
- Payoff consequence:
- Risk if too obvious:
- Risk if forgotten:
- Status: proposed / open / progressing / deferred / resolved / retired
```

Payoff rules:

- Answer one question while opening a deeper consequence.
- Make the earlier scene gain new meaning.
- Pay off with action, relationship change, status shift, or belief change, not only explanation.
- Do not hide information by making characters unnaturally silent.
- For serialized stories with many open promises, maintain the full Promise Debt Board and Current Hook Agenda in `serialization-governance.md`.

## Information Ledger

Use this for mystery, suspense, political plots, secrets, misunderstandings, betrayal, or unreliable narration.

```markdown
## Information State
| Item | Reader knows | Protagonist knows | Opponent knows | Other key character knows | Misread or lie | Planned reveal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Check before reveals:

- Does the reader have fair clues?
- Does a character's silence have a believable reason?
- Does revealing now create action, not only explanation?
- Does the reveal change how earlier events are understood?

## Core Cost Ledger

Use this whenever the story's central pull depends on an accumulating price: identity erosion, memory loss, fusion, corruption, dehumanization, illness, injury, debt, lifespan, magic cost, guilt, lost trust, or dwindling time.

First define what can be tracked:

```markdown
## Core Cost Mechanism
- What is being lost, altered, or consumed:
- Why the reader cares:
- Internal measure, if any:
- Sensory or behavioral evidence:
- Witness who notices:
- Artifact or record that preserves change:
- Reversible boundary:
- Irreversible threshold:
- Final feared state:
```

Then update it chapter by chapter:

```markdown
| Chapter | Quantified state, if applicable | Perceptible symptom | Triggering loss event | Witness reaction/record | Consequence now | Future risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Rules:

- Use numbers only when the story has established a credible measurement; pair them with lived consequence.
- Make each major increase matter in behavior, relationship, choice, capability, or memory.
- Let witnesses record loss differently: denial, documentation, fear, tenderness, exploitation, or misreading.
- Do not repeat the same symptom merely to remind the reader; escalate, transform, or recontextualize it.
- Retention may depend less on victory than on the reader asking how much of the character remains.

## Key Object Ledger

For important objects, use the full template in `project-state-and-production.md`. At minimum, track:

```markdown
| Object | Class/function | Current holder/location | Condition | Who knows its meaning | Setup/payoff status | Relationship/theme meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Key objects include evidence, weapons, medicines, keys, devices, notebooks, gifts, relics, keepsakes, identity proofs, and recurring symbolic items. Track transfer, damage, concealment, loss, return, and changed meaning. Do not classify autonomous beings as objects.

## Ability And Item-Effect Ledger

Use this when characters have skills, techniques, cultivation arts, supernatural powers, class abilities, equipment effects, unlocks, upgrades, cooldowns, charges, or counters. This is distinct from the key-object ledger: the object ledger tracks custody and meaning; this ledger tracks executable capability and its limits.

```markdown
## Ability System Rules
- Source/acquisition:
- Growth or unlock rule:
- Activation and failure cost:
- How capability level becomes perceptible:
- Hard boundary against sudden solutions:
- Major counters or vulnerabilities:

## Character Skills
| Ability | User | Source | Current level | Trigger | Effect boundary | Cost | Limitation/counter | Who knows | Next showcase/payoff |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Item Effects
| Object | Active/passive effect | Activation | Holder/bound user | Charges/durability/cooldown | Cost/side effect | Hidden effect/reveal window | Forbidden overreach |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Chapter Use Log
| Chapter | Ability/item effect used | Prior setup | Reader reward | Cost/consequence | New ability state | Future constraint | Accepted? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
```

Rules:

- Never allow a decisive new skill, upgrade, or item effect to appear only because the chapter needs an escape.
- Clever application may be left to prose execution only inside already established effect boundaries.
- Upgrade, unlock, and counter-reveal beats need setup, cause, cost, consequence, and write-back after acceptance.
- Ability display should produce story movement: a choice, exposure, relationship shift, cost, status change, promise payoff, or stronger threat.

## Resource And Subplot Tracking

Use the Countable Resource Ledger in `serialization-governance.md` when quantities, depletion, access, countdowns, favors, or measurable costs constrain choices. This is separate from key objects: a named notebook is an object; remaining medicine doses, money, or fusion percentage are resources.

Use the Subplot Board in `serialization-governance.md` when a side line promises later consequence and must not vanish between main-plot chapters.

```markdown

| Beat | Location | Surface action | Meaning now | Changed meaning later |
```

Example pattern: the same action appears as courtesy, then test, then refusal, then farewell. The object is not just a clue; it carries relationship and theme.
