# Prose Execution Guide

## Blueprint Translation

Translate planning language into dramatic behavior:

| Blueprint Language | Weak Prose | Stronger Prose Move |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Character notices a change | State the realization | Show a familiar routine failing, a response arriving late, or a wrong answer landing too calmly |
| Relationship becomes colder | Explain distance | Make one character choose a practical action over an old tenderness |
| Plant foreshadowing | Spotlight the clue | Let the clue enter as useful, funny, ordinary, or emotionally loaded before it matters |

| Show fear | Name fear | Make the body choose before the mind admits it |

## Scene Motion

Each scene should have:

- Entry disturbance.
- Character desire or pressure.
- Obstacle or mismatch.
- Tactic.
- Turn.
- Exit state different from entry state.

Do not treat a location change or conversation topic as a scene turn. Something must become harder, clearer, closer, more dangerous, more intimate, more costly, or more irreversible.

## Sentence And Paragraph Behavior

- Let each paragraph carry one pressure movement or perceptual unit.
- Vary sentence length by emotional and action rhythm, not decorative pattern.
- Use short sentences for impact only when the surrounding rhythm earns them.
- Prefer concrete verbs and charged objects over abstract emotion labels.
- Avoid explaining a metaphor after using it.
- Do not fill with generic atmosphere. Weather, light, sound, and texture must affect choice, perception, pressure, or mood.

## Dialogue

Good dialogue is not information exchange. It should carry at least one of:

- A tactic.
- A withheld fact.
- A status move.
- A misreading.
- A relationship test.
- A rhythm break.
- A wound, apology, lure, refusal, or trap.

Avoid letting characters speak the outline, theme, or backstory in a clean paragraph unless the scene makes that speech costly and biased.

## Open Creative Slots

When the blueprint leaves an open slot, invent:

- A sharper path to the fixed outcome.
- A small obstacle that reveals character.
- A minor object interaction that echoes theme without becoming a new plot device.
- A moment of silence, failed joke, wrong habit, sensory misfire, or social detail.

Do not invent:

- New long-term promises.
- New powers, major objects, or secret histories.
- New relationship turns not in the handoff.
- A different ending hook.
